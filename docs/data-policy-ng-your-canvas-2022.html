National Gallery Audience Survey (Your canvas) Data Policy 2022

This Privacy Policy explains who is processing your data, the data we collect, how we use it, who we share it with, and what your legal rights are.

Who is the Data Controller?

 The Office of Future Interactions, is the controller of personal data provided by respondents to the survey.

Our postal address is:

The Office of Future Interactions, Cambridge House, 16 High Street, Saffron Walden, Essex, UK, CB10 1AX You can contact us by email at: data@future-interactions.com

What are we collecting and how?

Responding to this survey is entirely voluntary.

We are collecting audience information comprising regional location, age group, visiting habits and subject interests regarding the National Gallery.

This information is submitted by participants through an online questionnaire.

Why are we collecting this data?

The responses you provide will be used to understand audience demographics, behavior, interests, experiences and preferences with regards to The National Gallery.

Asking about your experiences now helps us to give all National Gallery visitors the best possible experience in the future.

How will the data be used?

The Office of Future Interactions will use all survey responses for research purposes and to produce anonymous, statistical research findings and insight.

You will NOT be identifiable in any published results.

How long will we keep the data? Your personal data will not be kept for longer than is necessary to fulfill the specific purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, and to allow us to comply with our legal requirements. Any personally identifiable information will be deleted after a period of 30 days. The data we do hold onto will be anonymous and will be used to analyze to understand audience demographics, behavior, interests, experiences and preferences with regards to The National Gallery, but without any identifiable personal information. How secure is the data in our possession? The Office of Future Interactions takes its information security responsibilities seriously and applies various precautions to ensure your information is protected from loss, theft or misuse. Security precautions include secure data storage controlled and limited access to computer systems. Who will we share your data with? The Office of Future Interactions will share the data collected in this survey with The National Gallery. We will not share your information with any third parties for the purposes of direct marketing. Your Rights To request access to personal data that we hold about you, you should submit your request in writing to the e-mail address or postal address shown above. You have the following rights in relation to your personal data: Right to change your mind and to withdraw your consent Right to access your personal data Right to rectify your personal data Right to erase your personal data from our systems, unless we have legitimate interest reasons for continuing to process the information Right to port your personal data (portability right) Right to restrict processing of your personal data Right to object to the processing of your personal data Right to not be discriminated against for exercising any of the rights available to you under applicable data protection laws.
